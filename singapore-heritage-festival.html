SINGAPORE HERITAGE FESTIVAL 2019

In celebration of Singapore’s Bicentennial, the 16th edition of the Singapore Heritage Festival (SHF) which took place from 15 March to 7 April across four weekends, featured themes and programmes that explored and commemorated the occasion. Organised by the National Heritage Board, the festival provided a fresh look at the island’s history, allowing Singaporeans to uncover hidden stories and explore pivotal moments, communities and people that have shaped our nation.

Jerome Lim from The Long and Winding Road giving a guided tour of the former View Road Hospital

The Singapore Land Authority (SLA) supported SHF 2019 with a series of hospital tours, which were part of its main series of State property tours titled Discovering Singapore’s Best Kept Secrets. The SLA’s hospital tours featured visits to 10 View Road, formerly known as Woodbridge Hospital, as well as the former St Andrew’s Mission Hospital at 5 Kadayanallur Street and the popular Old Changi Hospital.

Participants on the tour to Old Changi Hospital

On top of the SLA’s hospital tours, SHF 2019 also featured travelling exhibitions, bus tours and activities across the island that told the lesser-known stories of Singaporeans’ shared spaces and way of life.
